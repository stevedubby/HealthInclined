## Hook
You stand up after sitting for a while and think, “Why do I feel stiff?” If your back, shoulders, or hips tighten right after you change positions, you’re not alone.

## Simple Explanation
When you sit, your muscles often stay in a “shortened” position for a long time. Less movement also means less gentle circulation and less nerve gliding in the area. That combo can leave you feeling tight or a bit uncomfortable.

## Common Causes
- Long stretches without moving
- Posture that puts your body in the same position (rounded shoulders, slouched hips, bent knees)
- Dehydration (which can make muscles feel less “comfortable”)
- Overdoing one activity earlier (like lifting, long walking, or workouts)
- Stress (tension builds up in the body even if you don’t notice)

## When It’s Normal vs When to Pay Attention
### Normal (usually)
- Tightness improves within minutes after standing or walking
- You feel “stiff” more than you feel sharp pain
- It shows up mostly after sitting or commuting

### Pay attention
- Pain that is sharp, severe, or steadily worsening
- Numbness, weakness, or pain that travels down a leg/arm
- Tightness after a fall/accident
- Fever, unexplained weight loss, or symptoms that don’t improve

## What You Can Do
- Do a “movement reset” every 30–45 minutes: stand up, roll shoulders, and walk for 1–2 minutes
- Try gentle stretches (no aggressive bouncing). Hold 15–30 seconds, repeat 2–3 times
- Add a short hip and chest mobility routine to your day (2–5 minutes)
- Drink water throughout the day and pay attention to how you feel
- Use a supportive seat: feet flat, hips not tucked too far under

## Quick Summary
Muscles often feel tight after sitting because they stay in the same position and move less. Break up sitting with light movement, gentle stretches, and hydration. If you have sharp pain, numbness, weakness, or symptoms that won’t improve, consider getting checked.
